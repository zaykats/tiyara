01

The Name

Every word has a history. Afaq is not a brand invention — it is a real Arabic and Darija word with roots in Moroccan everyday language and classical poetry alike.

آفاق
Classical Arabic — plural of ufuq (أُفُق), meaning a single horizon. Afaq refers to many horizons — the full 360° view from altitude.
AFAQ
In Moroccan Darija, afaq is the word used daily for horizon and for the future. "L-mustaqbal w-l-afaq" — the future and the horizons — is a common expression.
LANGUAGE
Classical Arabic & Moroccan Darija. Understood across all Arabic-speaking regions and North Africa without translation.
WHY THIS WORD
Three reasons it works for this document: First, aviation is the literal domain of horizons — every flight has one. Second, the document is about what Tiyara can become — its own horizons. Third, it ties the product to its Moroccan origin without needing an explanation.
RELATIONSHIP
Tiyara (طيارة = aircraft / aviation) is the product. Afaq is the document series brand sitting inside it — the vision arm of the platform identity.
PRONUNCIATION
AH-faq  /  آفاق  —  stress on the first syllable. Clear in English, French, and Arabic.

ATLAS AMBER
The primary accent. Warm, authoritative, decisive. Used for calls-to-action, reference numbers, and the right half of the horizon gradient. It is the color of first light on the Atlas peaks.
SKY BLUE
Secondary accent. Used for labels, technical monospace elements, the left half of the gradient. Signals precision and altitude. Not corporate blue — specifically aviation blue.
HORIZON GRADIENT
Sky → Mist → Amber. This gradient is the single most important visual element of the identity. It should appear only as a line — never as a fill or background wash.
NIGHT
The base. Every screen and dark-mode document uses this as background. It is not pure black — it has a very slight blue cast that feels like depth rather than void.

06

What Not To Do

Six rules. Breaking any of them disconnects the mark from the identity.

DO NOT — stretch or distort
AFAQ
The proportions of the letterforms are fixed. Scaling non-uniformly distorts the geometric nature of Space Grotesk.
DO NOT — recolor the wordmark
AFAQ
The wordmark is white on dark or black on light. The gradient belongs only to the horizon line — never applied to the letterforms.
DO NOT — use a different typeface
AFAQ
The mark is inseparable from Space Grotesk. Any other typeface — serif, script, or system font — is a different identity.
DO NOT — replace the horizon line with a shape
AFAQ
No circles, polygons, wings, or plane icons. The horizon line is the only graphic element the identity uses.
DO NOT — use on a busy background
AFAQ
The logo requires a solid or very clean background. Patterns, photos, and busy gradients destroy the contrast and legibility.
DO NOT — remove the Arabic
AFAQ
The Arabic script is not optional decoration. It is part of the identity. Removing it strips the word of its origin and meaning.

This identity is part of the Tiyara brand system. It should be used exclusively for the innovation, vision, and strategy document series. For the product UI, refer to the Tiyara main design system.
